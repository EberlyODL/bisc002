(aka Incomplete Dominance)

A different type of allele interaction involves a blending of the traits of each allele. Rather than having a dominant allele that masks the recessive one, in Semi Dominance or Incomplete Dominance the heterozygous combination give a third phenotype.

For example, in a flower, the petal color may have red as the dominant trait and white as the recessive trait. Normally a heterozygous plant (Aa) would have the recessive trait masked. Instead, in this case, the heterozygous gives a new phenotype, the flower is pink, a mixing of the red and white pigments.

Another example could be dark red and pale cherries. The dark color and the pale color would be seen in the homozygous phenotypes. However a heterozygous plant would produce pink cherries, a true blend of the dark and pale colors.
