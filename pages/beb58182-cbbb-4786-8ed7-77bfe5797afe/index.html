Bats are more closely related to whales than birds. This would not make sense unless the whale and bat have a more recent common ancestor than the bat and a bird. By studying the differences between species based on genetics we can tell how closely related two organisms are. The more differences we see, the less closely related they are.

Phylogenetic trees

Based on these differences we can build a tree like structure to show how closely related organisms are and see where in history they have both had a common ancestor. This tree shows the relationship between all life on earth.

This tree on the other hand shows the branching relationships among mammals.

There was a parting of the ways when mammals branched off from other creatures like the fish. The branch to the right shows fish evolution that culminates in sharks. Mammals took the left branch. However mammals then split into placental mammals and marsupials. The marsupial branch is shown here to cumulate in the marsupial wolf. The rest shows other branching leading to dogs, seals, cows and whales. Note whales are mammal and even though they look a bit like a shark and swim in the ocean they are only distantly related to sharks.
