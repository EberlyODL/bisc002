Is the manipulation of genes in living organisms new?  NO!

  • selective breeding programs have existed since before the age of recorded history
  • breeds of dogs, cats and farm animals have been "created" by people
  • simply have animals with desirable traits have offspring
  • can do the exact same thing with plants

Public concern and GMO's

  • There is an increased sensitivity about the safety of these plants
  • Plants have been genetically modified, are they harmful now?
    • No.  Tremendous research is involved to ensure safety.
  • Common worries
    • Toxic foods (allergies)
    • Gene transfer from one plant to another
    • Loss of biodiversity (ecological devastation)
  • This concern is especially relevant in Europe
  • Sensitive about food safety issues due to Mad Cow Disease

GMO vs. "Natural" breeding

  • selectively breeding 2 organisms may result in new phenotype
  • will result in thousands of genotypic changes
  • genetic engineering results in change of a single genotype (insert a gene)
  • will give the desired phenotype
  • genes are well characterized and function is well known

Overall, genetic engineering is LESS likely to lead to unknown gene effects
