The process of DNA replication begins with the separating of the two strands of DNA by an enzyme called Helicase. Another enzyme called Primase inserts a short piece of preformed DNA called a Primer. The primer is necessary for other enzymes that need to assemble a new chain of nucleotides.

Once the primers are in position a DNA Polymerase enzyme moves along the exposed single strand of DNA and “reads” the letters on that strand. The polymerase then inserts the proper nucleotide letter into position on the new matching strand. It continues to do this until the entire strand is copied.
