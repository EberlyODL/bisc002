Punnett Squares are patterns that can be determined when focusing on only one characteristic at a time. Such a cross is said to be Monohybrid. An example of a Monohybrid pattern can be seen in peas below.

However, it’s possible to track two or even more characteristics simultaneously. The following is an image of a dihybrid cross.

In this example two traits are shown, tail length and victory status. As you can see, the square is much larger because there are more combinations possible. While still following the dominance rules as before there are 16 possible genotypes but only 4 phenotypes. Since many traits (like height, weight or intelligence) involve many genes it’s easy to see why humans exhibit such a wide variety of phenotypes in any large group of people.
