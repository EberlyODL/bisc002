Of these major extinctions a few are worthy of special note. The 3rd great extinction occurred at the end of the Permian era. This was the largest of all the mass extinctions. Somewhere in the region of 95% of all species in existence at the time became extinct. This was a time of great change as the continents were merging together to form one huge continent, Pangea.

As a result, with only one large landmass, the rest of the planet was one huge ocean. Since most of the life on the planet is found near the coasts, a merging of landmass leads to a reduction in coastal habitats and a reduction in the number of organisms present. The loss of many shallow seas in favor of the formation of a huge deep ocean caused many global climactic changes. Such changing times had a profound impact on the climate and it's this rapid climate change that led to the loss of most organisms in existence at the time.
