A commonly misunderstood phrase is "Survival of the fittest". It really means that for a given situation, some individuals are more "fit" or able to survive and pass on these useful traits to offspring. This is a form of differential reproduction since only a few individuals will be able to have offspring. In the end the population will eventually contain a collection of individuals best suited to survive a specific, current environment.

Traits will be preserved in offspring in proportion to their adaptive benefits. One common example is the use of camouflage which helps an animal escape a predator. For example, a snowshoe hare has white fur which provides it with good camouflage where there is lots of snow present (tundra/Arctic environments). However, in a wooded environment like here in Pennsylvania, a white rabbit would stand out in the brown or green forests and fields, and it LESS likely to survive long enough to pass on the white fur trait to offspring. We say that the environment puts a selective pressure on those individuals not well adapted to survive in that environment
