Meiosis is the process of forming haploid gamete cells, sperm or egg cells used in reproduction. A single diploid cell duplicates its DNA and produces four haploid cells. The process is similar to Mitosis in that the cell divides but in Meiosis, the cell then goes into a second round of division without replicating the chromosomes. Therefore the resultant cells have half the normal number of a diploid cell. They are haploid rather than diploid.
