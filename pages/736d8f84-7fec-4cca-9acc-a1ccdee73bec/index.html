With more work on the other observable traits of pea plants Mendel was able to gradually work out some rules that inheritance of traits had to follow. He was able to predict with a high degree of statistical validity exactly what each generation would produce. Mendel found that physical characteristics seemed to come in pairs. However when plants having different forms of the trait are cross pollinated, only one of the two forms appears in the offspring. One trait is dominating the other one. The other trait, the recessive one is masked but still present. This rule of dominance is a common feature of many genetic characteristics.

Mendel had discovered that for any given characteristic or gene there can be different forms. The term Allele is used to describe the alternative forms of a gene.
