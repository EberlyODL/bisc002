Before discussing the process of Translation, there are a few more parts to the assembly process that need to be discussed. A ribosome is the assembly factory for proteins. Here the mRNA is translated and amino acids are physically connected to each other to form a protein.

A ribosome is like pair of shells that fit on top of each other . There is a top subunit and a bottom subunit that fit together onto an mRNA strand. In addition the ribosome has two holes drilled into the top if it. One is called the A site and the other is the P site. There are the locations where amino acids will be placed in order to hook them together into a chain.

Another important assembly component is the tRNA or transfer RNA. This is a single strand of RNA that forms a particular cloverleaf type shape. On one end is a set of 3 nucleotides called the Anticodon. This set of 3 will base pair to a specific codon on the mRNA. At the other end of the tRNA is attached a specific amino acid.
